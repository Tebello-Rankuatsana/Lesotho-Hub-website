Organic Farming

What is Organic Farming?

Organic farming is a method of farming that avoids chemicals like fertilizers and pesticides. It relies on natural processes, compost, and manure.

Keys Features:

1.No genetically modified organisms.

2.Improves soil fertility naturally.

3.Produces healthier, chemical-free food.

Agroforestry

What is Agroforestry?

A land use system that combies agriculture and forestry by growing trees alongside crops or livestock.

Key Features:

1.Trees provide shade and reduce erosion.

2.Enhances biodiversity.

3.Improves water retention and soil fertility.

Dairy farming

What is Dairy Farming?

Farming that focuses on producing milk and other products such as cheese, butter, and yohgurt.

Key Features:

1.Involves rearing cows, goats, or buffalo.

2.Requires proper feeding, housing, and animal healthcare.

3.Often includes milking equipment and storage facilities.

Livestock Farming

What is Livestock Farming

Raising animals for food e.g., meat, milk,eggs and labor as well as a source of resources e.g., wool, leather.

Key Features:

1.Requires grazing land or feeding systems.

2.Needs veterinary care and housing.

3.Herds of common animals:pigs, goats, cow etc.

Crop Farmers

What is Crop Farming?

The cultivation of plants for food,feed, or raw materials.

Key Features:

1.Requires soil management

2.Can be subsistence or commercial

3.Mostly includes maize, wheat, vegetables etc.

Poultry Farming

What is Poultry Farming?

Raising birds for meat or eggs.

Key Features:

1.Requires proper housing

2.Regular vacination of birds

3.Ranges of small scale to industrial scale
